New Hampshire just rejected a $100 million Bitcoin-backed bond, a decision that could have far-reaching implications for the use of cryptocurrency in government finance. The use of Bitcoin as collateral for bonds is still a relatively new concept, and this rejection suggests that there is still a lot of work to be done to convince governments that it's a viable option.

The rejection of the Bitcoin-backed bond in New Hampshire is a major blow to the idea of using cryptocurrency in government finance, but it's not the only big news in the crypto world this week. It's definitely something to keep an eye on in the coming weeks and months.

As we continue to navigate the ever-changing landscape of cryptocurrency, it's also worth noting that JPMorgan is warning that the real threat to Bitcoin isn't MicroStrategy's sales, but rather private blockchains.
