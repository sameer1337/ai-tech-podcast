Crypto Regulatory Shifts

South Korea and UK lead global crypto regulatory changes

🕔 2026-07-20·Crypto Daily Brief
▶ Listen · 5 min

As the global crypto market continues to evolve, regulatory bodies are taking notice and implementing changes to ensure a safe and secure environment for investors. According to Cointelegraph.com News, South Korea is eyeing a September launch for the second phase of its CBDC pilot, which will add two regional banks, new payment features, and tests of government subsidy payments using tokenized bank deposits. This development has significant implications for the future of digital currencies and how they are regulated. The Bank of Korea’s CBDC pilot is a crucial step towards understanding the potential benefits and drawbacks of central bank-issued digital currencies.

The introduction of new payment features and the testing of government subsidy payments using tokenized bank deposits will provide valuable insights into the practical applications of CBDCs. As the world watches the development of South Korea’s CBDC pilot, it is essential to consider the broader context of crypto regulation. The Virtual Asset User Protection Act, enacted two years ago, has led to a significant increase in crypto-related investigations, with 40 cases of crypto manipulation probed over the past two years, as reported by Cointelegraph.com News.

This increased scrutiny highlights the need for clear and effective regulation in the crypto space. The Financial Services Commission’s efforts to combat crypto manipulation demonstrate the importance of regulatory bodies in maintaining a fair and transparent market. As the crypto market continues to grow and evolve, it is crucial that regulatory bodies stay ahead of the curve and provide clear guidance on the rules and regulations governing digital assets.

Regulatory Developments in South Korea

The second phase of South Korea’s CBDC pilot is a significant development in the country’s crypto regulatory landscape. The addition of two regional banks and new payment features will expand the scope of the pilot and provide more comprehensive testing of the CBDC’s capabilities. As reported by Cointelegraph.com News, the pilot will also test government subsidy payments using tokenized bank deposits, which could have significant implications for the future of social welfare programs.

The success of the CBDC pilot will depend on various factors, including the effectiveness of the new payment features and the ability of the tokenized bank deposits to facilitate government subsidy payments. The Bank of Korea will need to carefully evaluate the results of the pilot and make any necessary adjustments to ensure the smooth implementation of the CBDC. The introduction of a CBDC could also have significant implications for the traditional banking system, as it could potentially disrupt the existing payment infrastructure.

As the world watches the development of South Korea’s CBDC pilot, it is essential to consider the potential risks and benefits associated with the introduction of a central bank-issued digital currency. The CBDC could provide a more efficient and secure way of making transactions, but it also raises concerns about privacy and the potential for government control over the financial system. The Bank of Korea will need to carefully balance these competing interests and ensure that the CBDC is designed and implemented in a way that benefits all stakeholders.

Contextually, the development of CBDCs is part of a broader trend towards the digitization of currencies. Many countries are exploring the potential benefits of digital currencies, including increased efficiency, security, and financial inclusion. However, the introduction of a CBDC also raises complex questions about the role of central banks in the digital economy and the potential impact on traditional banking systems.

Global Regulatory Trends

The UK has recently introduced a new regulation that makes delayed wallet identification a 14-year criminal risk for crypto firms, as reported by CryptoSlate. This development highlights the increasing scrutiny of crypto firms by regulatory bodies and the need for clear and effective regulation in the industry. The regulation, which took effect on July 17, has significant implications for UK-linked crypto firms, which will need to reconstruct what they knew and when to avoid potential criminal liability.

The introduction of this regulation demonstrates the UK’s commitment to combating money laundering and terrorist financing in the crypto space. However, it also raises concerns about the potential impact on innovation and the ability of crypto firms to operate effectively. The UK will need to balance its regulatory approach to ensure that it provides a safe and secure environment for investors while also promoting innovation and growth in the crypto industry.

The global crypto regulatory landscape is becoming increasingly complex, with different countries and jurisdictions taking varying approaches to regulating digital assets. The US is currently considering the CLARITY Act, which could provide much-needed clarity on the regulation of crypto assets. However, the fate of the act is uncertain, and it remains to be seen whether it will be passed into law.

As the global crypto regulatory landscape continues to evolve, it is essential for investors and crypto firms to stay up-to-date with the latest developments and trends.
