AI Confidence Drops

Six months ago, 40% of IT leaders described their organizations as mature in AI deployment, but today that number is 23%, as reported by VentureBeat. This shift in confidence might reflect a more realistic understanding of AI capabilities and limitations. The data from a recent survey of 800 IT leaders across the U.S. and U.K. tells a consistent story: the organizations revising their AI strategies are the ones most likely to achieve success.

In context, the drop in AI confidence can be seen as a natural consequence of the industry's rapid growth. Hugging Face's Security Breach

Hugging Face, a leading AI company, recently experienced a security breach, as reported by VentureBeat. The incident response team first turned to frontier AI models to analyze the breach, but the models refused to help. Commercial safety guardrails built to stop attackers blocked every forensic query because they treated the IR team's real exploit data the same way they would treat a live attack.

The attacker, an autonomous AI agent, moved laterally across the Hugging Face infrastructure for a weekend, undetected and unstopped.
