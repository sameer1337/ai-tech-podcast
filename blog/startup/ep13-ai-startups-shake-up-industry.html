AI startups shake up industry

SpaceX, Slack, and OpenAI launch new AI products, Anthropic expands to mobile and web

🕔 2026-07-09·Startup Wire Daily
▶ Listen · 5 min

Artificial intelligence startups are making waves in the industry with new product launches and expansions. SpaceX's Grok 4.5, Slack's Slackbot integration, OpenAI's GPT-Live, and Anthropic's Claude Cowork are just a few examples of the innovative solutions being developed. These advancements are not only improving the capabilities of AI models but also increasing their accessibility and reach.

SpaceX's Grok 4.5 Launch

According to VentureBeat, SpaceX's Grok 4.5 is the first artificial intelligence model the company has trained specifically for coding and autonomous agents. This launch marks a significant test of the company's AI empire, which was assembled over the past six months. The strategy behind Grok 4.5 is to provide a more affordable and efficient solution for developers, who may prioritize speed and cost over benchmark leaderboards.

The launch of Grok 4.5 is also a result of SpaceX's $60 billion acquisition of AI coding startup Cursor, completed just weeks ago. This acquisition has enabled SpaceX to expand its AI capabilities and develop more advanced models like Grok 4.5. As reported by VentureBeat, the model is priced at half the cost of its rivals, making it an attractive option for developers.

The impact of Grok 4.5 on the industry will be significant, as it has the potential to disrupt the dominance of Anthropic and OpenAI. As the AI landscape continues to evolve, it will be interesting to see how these companies respond to the launch of Grok 4.5. With its competitive pricing and advanced capabilities, Grok 4.5 is poised to make a significant impact on the market.

In the context of the AI industry, the launch of Grok 4.5 highlights the increasing importance of affordability and efficiency in AI model development. As more companies enter the market, the competition for developers' attention will continue to grow, driving innovation and advancements in the field.

Slack's Slackbot Integration

VentureBeat reports that Slack's Slackbot can now pull CRM data, generate charts, and send DocuSigns, all from a chat message. This integration connects Slackbot to the entire Salesforce platform, including CRM data, Tableau analytics, and Data 360 customer profiles. The mechanism behind this expansion is a set of dedicated Model Context Protocol (MCP) integrations.

The integration of Slackbot with Salesforce marks a significant milestone in the development of the two products as a single system. Five years after Salesforce acquired Slack, the two companies are finally starting to function as a unified platform. This integration has the potential to increase productivity and efficiency for users, as they can now access a wide range of tools and data from a single conversational prompt.

The impact of this integration on the industry will be significant, as it sets a new standard for collaboration and productivity tools. As more companies adopt similar integrations, the landscape of the industry will continue to evolve, with a focus on seamless communication and data sharing. With the rise of remote work, the importance of such integrations will only continue to grow.

In the context of the collaboration and productivity tools market, the integration of Slackbot with Salesforce highlights the increasing demand for unified platforms. As companies continue to adopt remote work arrangements, the need for efficient and integrated tools will drive innovation and advancements in the field.

OpenAI's GPT-Live Launch

According to VentureBeat, OpenAI has launched GPT-Live, a pair of new voice models that fundamentally redesign how people talk to ChatGPT. The two models, GPT-Live-1 and GPT-Live-1 mini, are rolling out globally and replace the company's existing Advanced Voice Mode with an architecture that can listen and speak simultaneously, much like an actual human conversation.

The launch of GPT-Live marks a significant advancement in the development of conversational AI models. With its ability to listen and speak simultaneously, GPT-Live has the potential to revolutionize the way people interact with AI models. The impact of this launch on the industry will be significant, as it sets a new standard for conversational AI and raises the bar for competitors.

The development of GPT-Live is a result of OpenAI's continuous efforts to improve the capabilities of its AI models. Anthropic's Claude Cowork Expansion

VentureBeat reports that Anthropic has launched Claude Cowork on mobile and web, expanding a tool that has quietly become the company's bridge between the developer-centric world of AI coding agents and the far larger market of knowledge workers who never open a terminal. The rollout, which begins in beta with Max subscribers, marks a strategic inflection for Anthropic.

The expansion of Claude Cowork to mobile and web is a significant development for Anthropic, as it transforms the tool from a desktop-only agent to a cross-device platform. This expansion has the potential to increase the adoption of Claude Cowork, as users can now access the tool from a variety of devices and platforms.

The impact of this expansion on the industry will be significant, as it highlights the increasing importance of accessibility and flexibility in AI model development. Lovable's Valuation

According to Startups | TechCrunch, Lovable is reportedly in talks to double its valuation to $13.2B. The $300 million round is expected to be led by Menlo Ventures, Sifted reported. This development is significant, as it highlights the growing interest in AI startups and their potential for growth and innovation.

The potential doubling of Lovable's valuation is a testament to the company's growth and potential.
