AI Security Risks

Enterprises face significant AI security gaps, while AI advancements continue to accelerate

🕔 2026-07-17·AI Tech Daily
▶ Listen · 5 min
As the world becomes increasingly reliant on artificial intelligence (AI), the risks associated with this technology are becoming more apparent. A recent report has highlighted the agent security gap, where enterprises are struggling to contain the risks associated with AI agents. Meanwhile, advancements in AI continue to accelerate, with the release of the largest open-source AI model to date.

Agent Security Gap

The agent security gap is a significant concern for enterprises, with 54% of those surveyed having already experienced an AI agent incident, according to a report by VentureBeat. This is largely due to the fact that most enterprises are still using shared credentials for their AI agents, rather than giving each agent its own scoped identity. Furthermore, only three in ten enterprises isolate their highest-risk agents, leaving them vulnerable to potential security breaches. As reported by VentureBeat, the security stack for AI agents is often borrowed from model providers and hyperscalers, rather than being purpose-built for agents. The lack of investment in AI agent security is also a concern, with spending on this area remaining a thin slice of the overall security budget. As a result, many enterprises are unsure whether their defenses are keeping pace with AI-enabled attackers. According to VentureBeat, enterprises are evenly split on this issue, highlighting the need for greater investment in AI agent security. For context, AI agents are software programs that use machine learning and natural language processing to perform tasks, such as data analysis and customer service. The implications of the agent security gap are significant, with potential security breaches having serious consequences for enterprises. As reported by VentureBeat, the use of shared credentials and lack of isolation for high-risk agents leaves enterprises vulnerable to attacks. In order to mitigate these risks, enterprises must prioritize the development of purpose-built security stacks for AI agents and invest in the necessary security measures.
