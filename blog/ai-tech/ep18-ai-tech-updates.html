DeepSeek's recent price cut, as reported by VentureBeat, should have been a welcome move for enterprise AI vendors and developers. However, the reduced pricing has not automatically translated into healthier margins, according to VentureBeat. This is because while inference costs have decreased, agent systems are consuming tokens at a faster rate than prices are declining. As a result, the 100x problem remains, where the cost of using AI models is still prohibitively expensive for many businesses.

The reason behind this phenomenon lies in the economics of software development. For the last two decades, software economics has been dictated by the rule that infrastructure becomes cheaper every year, while applications become more capable. Waze's AI Makeover

Waze, the popular driving app, is getting an AI makeover, courtesy of Google's flagship AI assistant, Gemini. According to The Verge, the integration of Gemini into Waze will allow users to personalize their trips more effectively. The update includes four new features, two of which involve Gemini. Waze is also updating its conversation reporting feature, which was first introduced in 2024.

The integration of AI into Waze is a significant development, as it has the potential to revolutionize the way users interact with the app. By leveraging Gemini's capabilities, Waze can provide more personalized and accurate traffic updates, making it easier for users to navigate through congested roads. Social Media Restrictions

The European Union is weighing sweeping new restrictions on children's and teenagers' access to social media, including age limits, an outright ban, and phased access. According to The Verge, social media platforms could also be forced to prove their services are not harmful before young people are allowed to use them. European Commission President Ursula von der Leyen has stated that the proposed restrictions are aimed at protecting young people from the potential harms of social media.

The proposed restrictions are a significant development, as they have the potential to impact the way social media platforms operate in Europe.
