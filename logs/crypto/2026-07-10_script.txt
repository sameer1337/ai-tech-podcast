New Hampshire just rejected a $100 million Bitcoin-backed bond, a decision that could have far-reaching implications for the use of cryptocurrency in government finance. The rejection came after a public finance hearing on July 8, where officials ultimately decided that the risks associated with Bitcoin collateral were too great. This is a significant setback for proponents of Bitcoin-backed bonds, who had hoped that New Hampshire would pave the way for other states to follow suit. The use of Bitcoin as collateral for bonds is still a relatively new concept, and this rejection suggests that there is still a lot of work to be done to convince governments that it's a viable option.

The rejection of the Bitcoin-backed bond in New Hampshire is a major blow to the idea of using cryptocurrency in government finance, but it's not the only big news in the crypto world this week.
