Bitcoin just slipped to $62,000, paring its recent rebound as CryptoQuant suggests there's still room for the cryptocurrency to move higher. This sudden drop is particularly noteworthy given that Bitcoin had been on a tear, rebounding from last week's $57,700 low. According to CryptoQuant, improving demand and seasonal trends could be the driving forces behind this potential upward movement. This is significant because it indicates that despite the volatility, there are still underlying factors that could push Bitcoin's price up. The fact that CryptoQuant sees room for growth is a beacon of hope for investors who have been waiting for a sign that the cryptocurrency is poised for a comeback.

But what's really interesting here is that this price movement is happening in a broader context where global events are also influencing the crypto market. For example, the US recently launched fresh strikes in Iran, which has been having a surprisingly muted impact on the price of Bitcoin and ether. This lack of reaction is notable, as you'd expect significant geopolitical events to send shockwaves through the market. Instead, both Bitcoin and ether have been little changed, suggesting that investors are either waiting to see how things play out or are simply not as spooked by global instability as they used to be.
