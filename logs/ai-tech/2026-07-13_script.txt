$12 billion in annual savings is what DeepSeek's 75% price cut on its V4-Pro model could bring to enterprise AI vendors and developers, but many are discovering that cheaper models don’t automatically translate into healthier margins. The reason is simple: while inference costs plummet, agent systems are voraciously consuming tokens faster than prices are declining. For the last two decades, software economics was dictated by a straightforward relationship between computing power and cost, but the rise of AI has turned this model on its head. Now, the 100x problem remains, where the cost of training and deploying AI models is exponentially higher than traditional software, and DeepSeek's price cut only scratches the surface of this issue.

This drastic price cut is a clear indication that the AI industry is shifting towards a more competitive landscape, where companies are forced to reduce prices to stay ahead of the game. However, the underlying issue of token consumption and the resulting costs will continue to plague developers and vendors, making it essential to find innovative solutions to mitigate these effects.
