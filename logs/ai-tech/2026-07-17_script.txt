54% of enterprises have already had an AI agent incident, with most still allowing these agents to share sensitive credentials, a staggering statistic that highlights the glaring security gap in the AI industry. This is according to a recent report that surveyed 107 enterprises, which found that more than half have experienced a confirmed AI agent security incident or a near-miss. The sheer scale of this problem is alarming, and it's clear that many companies are still playing catch-up when it comes to securing their AI systems. The report also found that only about a third of enterprises give every agent its own scoped identity, and most agents still share credentials, which is a recipe for disaster.

The fact that most enterprises are still using security stacks borrowed from model providers and hyperscalers, rather than purpose-built solutions for AI agents, is a major concern. It's no wonder that spending on AI agent security remains a tiny fraction of the overall security budget. As AI agents become increasingly ubiquitous, it's imperative that companies take a more proactive approach to securing them. The consequences of inaction could be disastrous, with potential breaches and data leaks waiting to happen. This is an area that needs urgent attention, and companies must start taking concrete steps to address the agent security gap.
