Alphabet Inc. just posted a strong earnings report, but it wasn't enough to calm investors' nerves as stocks fell 2.5% on growing fears over ever-higher artificial intelligence spending. This is a big deal because it shows that even when a major player like Alphabet beats expectations, the market is still skittish about the rising costs of AI. The report was solid, with revenue up 10% and profits beating analyst estimates, but the concern over AI spending is overshadowing all of that. It's not just Alphabet, either - the Nasdaq composite index is down 5% over the past week, and the Dow Jones Industrial Average is down 3%, as investors start to worry that the AI arms race is going to get expensive.

The reason this matters is that AI is becoming a major driver of costs for companies, and investors are starting to get nervous about how that's going to affect the bottom line. It's not just the direct costs of developing and implementing AI, either - it's also the costs of talent acquisition and retention, as companies compete for the top AI researchers and engineers. This is a trend that's going to continue, and investors need to be paying attention to how companies are managing their AI spending. Speaking of which, the Iran crisis is also heating up, and that's got investors on edge, especially when it comes to the Fed's next move.

The intensifying crisis in Iran has got the Fed watching closely, and Treasury yields are approaching the highest levels seen since the war started in February. This is a big deal because it increases the likelihood of a Fed rate hike, which could put a damper on the bull market. Oil prices are rising, too, with Brent crude climbing for a fifth straight day, and that's got investors worried about inflation and the impact on economic growth. The Fed is walking a tightrope here, trying to balance the need to control inflation with the need to keep the economy growing, and the Iran crisis is making that job a lot harder.
